## Anti–Intellectual Revolution
* **Component ID:** agendas_antiintellectual_revolution
* **Revision:** 0
* **Status:** Current
* **Source:** Base Game

## Classified Document Leaks
* **Component ID:** agendas_classified_document_leaks
* **Revision:** 0
* **Status:** Current
* **Source:** Base Game

### Clarification Notes
1. IfClassified Document Leaksis revealed when only one secret objective is in play, players may only vote for that secret objective or abstain. If no votes are cast, the speaker must decide for that secret objective to be elected.
2. The player that originally owned the secret objective elected byClassified Document Leakscannot score it again after it becomes a public objective.
3. The elected secret objective no longer counts towards the original player’s limit of three secret objectives.
4. IfClassified Document Leaksis discarded, the elected secret objective is not returned to the original player, and it is no longer scorable as a public objective. It remains in play, not discarded.
5. A player can score a maximum of one objective of any type during or after each combat. A player cannot score the elected objective and one of their own secret objectives from the same combat.
6. The elected objective becomes a public objective and stops being a secret objective.

## Enforced Travel Ban
* **Component ID:** agendas_enforced_travel_ban
* **Revision:** 0
* **Status:** Current
* **Source:** Base Game

### Clarification Notes
1. The effect of theEnforced Travel Banlaw does not affect the Creuss player.
2. Enforced Travel Banonly prevents movement using the normal adjacency provided by wormholes. Other effects that create adjacency, such as theWormhole Reconstructionagenda or theLost Star Chartaction card, may be used to allow movement between systems containing wormholes.
3. When theEnforced Travel Banlaw is in play, units cannot move through wormholes, even by effects that move ships outside of theMovementstep of a tactical action, such as while retreating.
4. When theEnforced Travel Banlaw is in play, hits may still be produced against ships in the active system by units with theDeep Space Cannonability in systems that are adjacent via a wormhole during theSpace Cannon Offensestep of a tactical action.
5. Players may still be neighbors via systems that are adjacent via wormholes.
6. The “Against” effect ofEnforced Travel Banwill affect PDS in or adjacent to systems containing an alpha, beta, delta or gamma wormhole.

## Miscount Disclosed
* **Component ID:** agendas_miscount_disclosed
* **Revision:** 0
* **Status:** Current
* **Source:** Base Game

### Clarification Notes
1. IfMiscount Disclosedis revealed when only one law is in play, players may only vote for that law or abstain. If no votes are cast, the speaker must decide for that law to be elected.
2. The elected law remains in play, and remains elected to the same object if applicable, while it is being voted on.

6. Players who resolved a transaction while voting on and resolvingMiscount Disclosedmay resolve an additional transaction voting on and resolving the elected law.
7. IfHoly Planet of Ixth,Shard of the ThroneorThe Crown of Emphidiais elected byMiscount Disclosed, the owner will not lose a victory point.

## Wormhole Research
* **Component ID:** agendas_wormhole_research
* **Revision:** 0
* **Status:** Current
* **Source:** Base Game

### Clarification Notes
1. A player with ships in a delta or gamma wormhole may research a technology from the “For” effect. Those ships will not be destroyed.
2. A player must still meet the prerequisites for the technology they research.
3. Ground forces in the space area of a system containing an alpha or beta wormhole will be removed due to lack of capacity after the ships in that system are destroyed; those ground forces will not trigger any “destroyed” effects. Fighters will be destroyed, and will trigger any “destroyed” effects.
4. IfAnti–Intellectual Revolutionis in play as a law, players who research a technology will destroy a ship due toAnti–Intellectual Revolutionbefore they destroy ships in systems containing alpha or beta wormholes. As such, they may choose a ship in a system containing an alpha or beta wormhole to destroy forAnti–Intellectual Revolution
5. The Saar player’s Floating Factories will not be destroyed byWormhole Research.
