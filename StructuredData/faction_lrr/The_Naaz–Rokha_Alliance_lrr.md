## Hesh and Prit — Perfect Synthesis
* **Component ID:** the_naazrokha_alliance_hesh_and_prit_perfect_synthesis
* **Component Type:** Hero

### Clarification Notes
1. The Naaz–Rokha player may choose to perform the secondary ability of zero, one or two strategy cards.
2. The Naaz–Rokha player cannot choose the same strategy card twice.
3. The Naaz–Rokha player must choose which strategy cards they will perform the secondary abilities of before they resolve any of those abilities.
4. When resolving the abilities of two strategy cards, they may be resolved in either order. Game objects affected by the first strategy card ability may be affected by the second.
5. For example, say the Naaz–Rokha player chooses to resolve the secondary abilities of theDiplomacyandWarfarestrategy cards. They may resolve theDiplomacyability first to ready some planets, then exhaust those same planets to pay for the units they produce with theWarfareability. Alternatively, they may resolve theWarfareability first, exhausting some of their readied planets to pay for the units they produce, then resolve theDiplomacyability to ready those same planets.
6. If a player is eliminated, the strategy cards that they had are considered “unchosen”.
7. If the Naaz–Rokha player has no command tokens in their reinforcements, they must use a command token from their command sheet when performing the secondary ability of the strategy cards.
8. If the Naaz–Rokha player has the Muaat player’sAlliancepromissory note, they will not gain a trade good from it when they use theirPerfect Synthesisability.

## Eidolon /Z–Grav Eidolon
* **Component ID:** the_naazrokha_alliance_eidolon_zgrav_eidolon
* **Component Type:** Mech

### Clarification Notes
1. The Naaz–Rokha mechs are only Z–Grav Eidolon during theSpace Combatstep, only in the active system, and only in the space area.
2. If the Naaz–Rokha player had  an Eidolon in the space area of the active system, they must mandatorily use its ability to convert it to a Z–Grav Eidolon.
3. All other players will have the opportunity to resolve an ability between the Naaz–Rokha player resolving their Eidolon ability and any other ability.
4. If the Naaz–Rokha player owns theAssault Cannontechnology, they may count their Z–Grav Eidolon towards the three non–fighter ships, once they resolve its ability. However, if another player then resolves an ability that reduces the number of non–ships the Naaz–Rokha player has in the active system to less than three, the Naaz–Rokha player will no longer be able to resolve theAssault Cannontechnology ability.
5. Any effect that may target ships or non–fighter ships may target a Z–Grav Eidolon.
6. A Z–Grav Eidolon is still a ground force and a mech.
7. A Z–Grav Eidolon participating in a space combat may count towards scoring theDemonstrate Your Powersecret objective.
8. When a Z–Grav Eidolon reverts back to an Eidolon at the end of combat, it will count towards capacity in the active system. Capacity limits must be met before proceeding to theInvasionstep.

## Absolute Synergy
* **Component ID:** the_naazrokha_alliance_absolute_synergy
* **Component Type:** Breakthrough

### Clarification Notes
1. The four mechs can be anywhere in the same system; they need not be all on the same planet, and some or all may be in the space area.
2. The mechs may be removed and the card flipped as soon as the four mechs are in the same system. For example, this may be done before space combat starts.

## Eidolon Maximum
* **Component ID:** the_naazrokha_alliance_eidolon_maximum
* **Component Type:** Mech

### Clarification Notes
1. When in the space area of a system, the Eidolon Maximum counts towards either of the Naaz–Rokha player’s capacity or fleet pool in that system.
2. If hits are produced by unit abilities against a group of the Naaz–Rokha player’s units that includes the Eidolon Maximum, then these hits must be assigned to the other eligible units in that group, if possible.
3. The Eidolon Maximum only repairs during combats that it is participating in.
4. The Naaz–Rokha player can never have two or more Eidolon Maximums on the game board.
5. The Eidolon Maximum is considered a mech for all relevant game effects, such as rolling an additional dice from the effect of theVisz El Vir.
6. The Eidolon Maximum is considered a ship for all relevant game effects.
7. The Eidolon Maximum may be destroyed by aDirect Hitaction card whenever it uses itsSustain Damageability, including if it does so during a ground combat.
