# Council Keleres
* **Document Type:** Faction LRR Notes

---

## The Tribunii
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_the_tribunii
* **Component Type:** Faction Ability

### Clarification Notes
1. The Keleres player chooses their starting technology after all other players, including the Argent, Bastion, Deepwrought, Firmament, Ral Nel, Rebellion, and Winnu players, have gained their starting technologies.
2. If there are not two unique starting technologies amongst those owned by other players, the Keleres player will start the game with fewer technologies. This will most commonly happen in a three player game, where one of the other players is playing as N’orr or Winnu.

## Council Patronage
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_council_patronage
* **Component Type:** Faction Ability

### Clarification Notes
1. The Keleres player must mandatorily replenish their commodities and gain a trade good every strategy phase.
2. If the Keleres player already has two commodities, they will still replenish their commodities during the strategy phase. This will not give them additional commodities, but will trigger any effects that happen when the Keleres player replenishes their commodities.
3. If another player is holding the Keleres player’sTrade Agreementpromissory note, they may use it when the Keleres player replenishes commodities fromCouncil Patronage.

## Law’s Order
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_laws_order
* **Component Type:** Faction Ability

### Clarification Notes
1. The blanked laws come back into effect at the end of the turn.
2. If this ability is used, all laws are blanked. The Keleres player cannot choose for some laws to remain in effect.
3. If the Keleres player owns thePolitical Censurelaw, they will not lose a victory point when it is blanked, and they may use action cards during this turn.
4. If the Keleres player then uses theRepeal Lawaction card, targeting thePolitical Censurelaw, then it will be discarded without the Keleres player losing the victory point.
5. The Keleres player and all of the other players treat all laws as blank for the turn.

## Keleres Rider
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_keleres_rider
* **Component Type:** Promissory Note

### Clarification Notes
1. After playingKeleres Rider, a player may use additional abilities, including playing other rider cards.
2. Any other riders may be played to predict the same or a different outcome.
3. The effect of a successfulKeleres Riderwill be resolved after the effect of the agenda.
4. A player who cannot vote may still play aKeleres Rider.
5. A player who cannot play action cards may still play aKeleres Rider.

## Agency Supply Network
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_agency_supply_network
* **Component Type:** Technology

### Clarification Notes
1. Any abilities that trigger “when/after a unit usesProduction” will trigger an additional time, for example, theSarween Toolstechnology.
2. The additionalProductionability cannot belong to the same unit as the originalProductionability.
3. During a tactical action, the active player must use everyProductionability of their units in the active system, or none of them. As such, the additionalProductionability must belong to a unit in a different system.
4. When the Keleres player resolves the secondary ability of the Warfare strategy card, they use theProductionability of a single space dock in their home system. As such, they may use theProductionability of a second unit in their home system, if present.
5. The Keleres player may use Custodia Vigilia as the additionalProductionability, and when they use Custodia Vigilia as the originalProductionability, they may use an additionalProductionability.
6. The originalProductionability must be fully resolved, before the Keleres player may use an additionalProductionability.
7. The Keleres player cannot pay one resource for two fighters or infantry, then place one with a unit using the originalProductionability and one with the unit using the additionalProductionability.

## Executive Order
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_executive_order
* **Component Type:** Technology

### Clarification Notes
1. If the Keleres player draws one of theClassified Document Leaks,Judicial Abolishment,Miscount DisclosedorNew Constitutionagendas, they will discard it if required and reveal a new agenda from the deck.
2. If theMiscount Disclosedagenda is resolved, the players will also vote on the elected agenda during the Keleres player’s action.
3. If thePublic Executionagenda is resolved, the elected player cannot vote in the agenda phase following the current action phase.
4. If an agenda would give multiple players a victory point, those players gain that victory point in initiative order, starting with the player with theLeadershipstrategy card (or next lowest initiative value should no player have theLeadershipstrategy card), regardless of the Keleres player’s initiative.
5. Other players may still perform one transaction total with the Keleres player during this turn, if they are neighbors.
6. Players exhaust planets to cast votes when resolvingExecutive Order, even if theRepresentative Governmentlaw is in play, which only affects voting during the agenda phase.
7. If a third law is put into play, theDictate Policysecret objective cannot be scored until the agenda phase.
8. TheDrive the Debatesecret objective cannot be scored while resolvingExecutive Order.
9. Effects from agenda outcomes that occur “at the end of this agenda phase” will instead resolve at the end of the next agenda phase.
10. Effects from agenda outcomes that occur “at the start of the next strategy phase” will still resolve at the start of the next strategy phase.

## Custodia Vigilia
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_custodia_vigilia
* **Component Type:** Legendary Planet

### Clarification Notes
1. The Custodia Vigilia planet card is gained exhausted.
2. Units cannot be placed on Custodia Vigilia.
3. Custodia Vigilia is not adjacent to any system, unit, player or other planet.
4. The Keleres player cannot lose control of Custodia Vigilia.
5. The Keleres player cannot be eliminated once they control Custodia Vigilia.
6. Custodia Vigilia may be used to qualify for objectives, if it meets any other requirements listed.
7. Custodia Vigilia cannot be used to qualify for any objective that requires planets in a system.
8. Custodia Vigilia may be elected by any “elect planet” agendas. However, Custodia Vigilia may be immune to the effects of the agenda.
9. A player gains victory points from the Imperial strategy card only if they control Mecatol Rex. If they use the Imperial strategy card to score an objective, then they gain victory points from that objective, and not the Imperial strategy card.
10. The Keleres player may use a command token to perform the secondary of Imperial during the same strategic action that they gained that token.
11. When the Keleres player uses theProductionability of Custodia Vigilia, it is treated as though the Keleres player was using theProductionabilitiy of one of their units on Mecatol Rex.
12. This may trigger the abilities of theSarween Toolstechnology, and other similar abilities.
13. Custodia Vigilia counts towards scoring theProduce en Masseobjective within the Mecatol Rex system.
14. The Ul player’sTerraformfaction promissory note may be attached to Custodia Vigilia.
15. IfTerraformis attached to Custodia Vigilia, it may be explored. However, Custodia Vigilia may be immune to the effects of some exploration cards.

## Suffi An
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_suffi_an
* **Component Type:** Commander

### Clarification Notes
1. The trade good to unlock Suffi An is spent after, and independent of, any trade goods spent while resolving the action card.
2. If the Keleres player uses their additional action to perform another component action, they may perform another additional action. They may do this any number of times.
3. Suffi An does not trigger from the component action of the action card used that was to unlock her.
4. If the Keleres player can perform a second action through some other game effect (such as theFleet Logisticstechnology), they may unlock Suffi An by playing an action card with a component action for their first action, then perform a second component action, which will then allow them to perform a third action because of Suffi An.
5. If a player’s turn is ended by a game effect, they cannot use Suffi An to perform an additional action.
6. A player cannot use Suffi An to pass on the same turn they performed an action.
7. If a player’s additional action would be to use a component action to play an action card, and that card is cancelled, they are not required to perform an alternate additional action on that turn.

## Omniopiares
* **Component ID:** council_keleres_omniopiares
* **Component Type:** Mech

### Clarification Notes
1. If a planet contains multiple mechs, the invading player must pay one influence for each, as separate payments. For example, if all four mechs were on a planet, the invading player could only exhaust Meer to pay for one instance of the ability; they would still have three instances of a single influence to pay.
